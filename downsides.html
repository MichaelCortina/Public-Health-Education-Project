The Downsides of Quarantines

 While quarantining is a very strong pandemic prevention strategy, it does not come without its downsides. In modern society, disease travels at a faster rate than ever before. This can decrease the effectiveness of a lockdown if the disease is able to spread rapidly before measures are taken (Taylor 2013). Additionally, many countries and provinces do not have well defined and patrolled borders, making it all the more difficult to impose a strict travel measures on the population (Chuengsatiansup and Limsawart 2019).

 Quarantine does not treat everyone equally. While many wealthier white-collar workers during the COVID-19 pandemic were able to maintain their jobs and switch to work from home, the same could not be said for many. The gig-economy, food and hospitality, and other jobs reliant on in person interaction were hit especially hard (Hegarty 2020). This compounds with the generally lower income of said workers, leading to a significant economic and social crisis for a very large portion of the workforce when implementing quarantine restrictions.

 The economic impact of quarantine is not only on the individual level. Due to globalism the world is more reliant and interconnected than ever before. As a result, when a country or region cuts itself off from the rest of the world during quarantine this has an enormous impact on the economy leading to an increase in wealth disparity (Ncube 2018). When in quarantine it becomes much harder for any economic growth to occur, and the supply of goods can often not reach consumer demand.

 The act of restricting freedoms taken for granted is also difficult, especially for a democratic government (Taylor 2013, 243). Restricting the rights of the people in quarantines for extended periods of time can lead to great social unrest. This can be exemplified in the recent nationwide protests China's Zero Covid Policy. These protests have caused China to back off on more intrusive measures such as forcing people into state quarantine camps (BBC News 2022). A country in quarantine must maintain the balance between protecting its populace from the disease, while allowing for personal freedoms. Strict quarantines become even harder to support as other countries begin to drop such measures.
