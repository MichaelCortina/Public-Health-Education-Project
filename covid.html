Quarantine In COVID19

 Quarantining was one of the first, and most effective disease prevention strategies used at the start, and throughout the COVID19 pandemic. When the novel coronavirus was first discovered in China, the quick action to quarantine the province of Wuhan, and prevent foreign travel is estimated to have reduced foreign exported cases by 70% to 77%. This lockdown additionally reduced case spread significantly from nearby provinces within China. It is estimated that between the initial lockdown and mid-February, travel bans prevented 73% of possible cases. This trend is continued in Australia, whose early lockdown is estimated to have prevented 80% of the cases early in the pandemic (Gr√©pin et al. 2021).

 This is in stark contrast to America's disjointed pandemic response. By placing the primary role of containing the virus on states instead of federal authority, response became split along party lines and inconsistent. This resulted in a lack of proper containment in the states and counties which needed it most (Altman 2020). If the federal government had taken greater control of the situation and imposed more strict lockdowns universally, significant numbers of cases and deaths could have been prevented.
